History

Whitewater rafting traces its roots back to the 1800s, when explorers first navigated wild rivers with rudimentary inflatable rafts. By the 1960s, rivers like the Colorado became popular for thrill-seekers, transforming rafting into a sport focused on adventure, discovery, and the beauty of uncharted waterways. This era saw significant advancements in rafting safety and equipment, enabling enthusiasts to tackle even the most challenging rapids, setting the stage for modern rafting companies to emerge.
